# Baguio City: A 25-Year Journey to the Summer Capital of the Philippines

For many Filipinos, Baguio is more than just a destination; it’s a dream. There’s a distinct magic in the air as you climb the winding roads of Kennon or Marcos Highway. Having visited this city over 20 times in the last 25 years, I’ve witnessed its evolution from a quiet pine-scented retreat to a bustling urban highland.



## The Eternal Spring: Life Without Air Conditioning
The most striking feature of Baguio remains its climate. Unlike the sweltering humidity of Manila or Cebu, Baguio offers a year-round "eternal spring." It is the only place in the Philippines where air conditioning is a luxury you simply don't need. The cool mountain breeze is nature’s own cooling system.

## Then vs. Now: The Reality of Modern Baguio
Looking back 25 years to my first visit, the changes are profound. The city has grown exponentially. While it’s heartening to see more diversity and international visitors, it has come at a cost: **Traffic.**

If you are planning a trip, here is my expert advice: **Avoid the year-end holidays.** During the peak season, the city becomes a pedestrian maze. Traffic is at a standstill, and catching a taxi is nearly impossible. To truly enjoy the city's soul, visit during the mid-week of the off-peak season.



## Must-Visit Spots: Curated by an Insider
If you find yourself in the City of Pines, here are the places that still hold their charm despite the urban growth:

### 1. Camp John Hay (The Peaceful Retreat)
The old American base remains the heart of Baguio's nostalgia. Walk through the **Yellow Trail** for a dose of pine-scented therapy or enjoy a cup of hot chocolate at **The Manor**. It’s the best place to escape the session road crowds.

### 2. BenCab Museum (Art in the Highlands)
Located a short drive from the city center, this museum houses the works of National Artist Benedicto Cabrera. The integration of indigenous Cordilleran art and modern masterpieces, set against a stunning mountain backdrop, is world-class.

### 3. Mines View Park & Good Shepherd
While crowded, the view of the Itogon mountains is iconic. Make sure to stop by the **Good Shepherd Convent** nearby to get their famous Ube Jam—just be prepared for the long queue!

### 4. Burnham Park (The Heart of the City)
A classic. Whether it's rowing a boat on the lagoon or biking under the morning sun, Burnham Park remains the quintessential Baguio experience for families and locals alike.

### 5. Session Road & Night Market
For those who love the urban vibe, Session Road offers a variety of local cafes. After 9:00 PM, part of the main road transforms into a **Night Market**, perfect for "Ukay-ukay" (thrift shopping) and street food.

## Final Thoughts
Baguio is a city of layers. Beyond the traffic and the crowds lies a cultural hub with a climate that heals. Having seen it change over two decades, I still find myself coming back. There’s always another cool morning and a fresh bowl of strawberry taho waiting at the top of the mountain.

**Ready to pack your sweater? Just remember to check the calendar before you book!**
